Hydrogen Fuel Cell Car

  • Gallery


Summary

The fuel cell car was designed to be an energy efficient autonomous vehicle that is able to navigate through an arbitrary white course marked with black guide lines. Optimization of the car included efficient algorithms to make well-informed decisions on forks and dead-ends, dynamic car designs, and proper cell maintenance.


Tech

Components:

TI MSP430 microcontroller • Pololu 6V motor driver • Two hydrogen fuel cells • QTR-1A Reflectance Sensors • DC motors • Touch sensors

Software:

C++ • IAR embedded workbench

Guitar Villain

  • Gallery


Summary

This project provides an alternative method to learning the basics of guitar playing. It is built in a similar fashion to the popular video game controller 'Guitar Hero', however in this version, the guitar can be set to an autonomous mode. Leap Maestro

  • Gallery


Summary

Maestro was created at Hack Western 2015. It uses the Leap Motion and Myo arm band to give the user the ability to control music playback and dynamics in real time; as if they were a conductor in an orchestra. Key features include:
• Dynamic playback at any speed subject to your conducting on the Leap Motion
• Ability to adjust equalization in real time using Myo
• Being able to do both in parallel , one on each hand
• Three JS 3D Model that tracks your movements
• Variety of song choices


Tech

Components:

Leap Motion • Myo Armband

Software:

Myo SDK • Leap Motion SDK • Web Audio API
Javascript: Node.js • Three.js • jQuery

Delta Scanner

  • Gallery

Summary

Delta Scanner was created at Delta Hacks 2015. It uses the Estimote BLE beacon to allow a user to trigger location-based events. The scanner uses position triangulation to accurately locate a device connected with a Bluetooth LE connection to an android application. With the intent of connecting to a smartphone that is carried around, a coffee maker was automated to turn on under specific time and movement conditions.


Tech

Components:

Three Estimote BLE beacon • Relays • Coffeemaker

Software:

Estimote Android SDK • Bluetooth LE API

Midnight Sun Solar Rayce Car Suspension Team

  • Gallery


Summary

The Midnight Sun Solar Rayce Car Team is the largest student run design team at the University of Waterloo. With mechanical, electrical, and business teams, the team is redesigns and builds a solar car biennially to compete in solar challenges such as the World Solar Challenge (Australia) and the American Solar Challenge (USA). With a completely new design, the MSXI features four wheels, two seats, trunk space, and a body made entirely out of carbon fiber and nomex. This redesign allows for testing of not only the speed of the car, but also its ability to perform in practical environments.


Key Responsibilities

• Machining of core components of solar vehicle
• Detecting and remodeling of design flaws using SolidWorks
• Working to manufacture and assemble suspension systems
